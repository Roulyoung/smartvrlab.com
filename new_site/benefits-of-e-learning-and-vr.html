What are the benefits of E-learning and VR

adminsvr · March 18, 2021
← Back to blog

Starting out with VR, we had no clearer picture of what advantages and disadvantages VR training had to offer than any other amateur. With experience, we now see where VR outperforms regular video for training. Here are the core benefits of combining e-learning with VR:

  • Possibility to repeat training
  • Cost savings (lower instructor time)
  • Consistent information delivery
  • No geographical limitations
  • Progress tracking analytics
  • High engagement rate
  • High retention rate

Below we compare classroom, e-learning, and VR to help you pick the right blend.

Alternatives to VR training

Every training approach has trade-offs across cost, quality, and learning goals.

Classroom learning

The most traditional format, with the benefit of live human interaction.

Advantages

  • Human interaction drives engagement and real-time feedback
  • Peer-to-peer learning expands perspectives
  • External motivation from being surrounded by learners
  • Content can be custom tailored on the spot

Disadvantages

  • No easy way to repeat sessions individually
  • Requires everyone to be in the same location
  • Instructor must attend (and be paid) every time
  • Inconsistency when instructors or groups change
  • Interaction depends on student willingness to participate

E-learning

Video/text-based courses serve many use cases well.

Advantages

  • Repeat training anytime; great for missed sessions
  • Instructor records once, reducing ongoing time/cost
  • Consistent content for every learner
  • Learning analytics to track progress
  • Mobile access (laptop, phone, desktop) from anywhere
  • Threaded Q&A so everyone sees answers
  • Self-paced: fast learners aren?t held back; others can take more time

Disadvantages

  • Limited live human interaction
  • Higher risk of procrastination without external motivation
  • Not instantly tailored to individual questions (unless updated later)

Virtual Reality training

VR shares e-learning strengths and adds immersion.

Advantages

  • All e-learning benefits (repeatability, consistency, analytics, mobility)
  • High engagement: studies show higher engagement vs lectures and video
  • High retention: research shows stronger long-term retention with VR

Disadvantages

  • Hardware availability/logistics, especially for distributed teams
  • Low-quality cardboard viewers reduce experience quality
  • Open-text input is still cumbersome; multiple choice works best today

Why integrate e-learning and VR now

E-learning plus VR delivers the most effective training mix in many business contexts. VR?s engagement and retention pair well with e-learning?s scalability and analytics.

The bottom line

The right blend of e-learning and VR gives you repeatable, consistent training with higher engagement and retention.
