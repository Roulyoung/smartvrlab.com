What VR can do: Making empathy easier

adminsvr · March 15, 2021
← Back to blog

Why empathy training in VR works

First-person VR can trick the brain into adopting a virtual body, triggering real responses to virtual events. That makes it a powerful tool for perspective-taking and empathy building.

Study: VR embodiment boosts empathy

An eNeuro study immersed participants in a scene of verbal abuse, embodied as the victim. Functional MRI showed that first-person embodiment increased identification with the virtual body.

What the brain data revealed

Participants showed synchronized activity in body ownership and personal space networks, plus heightened threat-response when the abuser approached. VR made the brain react as if the threats were real.

How to use VR for empathy training

Organizations can apply VR to offender rehabilitation, diversity and inclusion training, healthcare bedside manner, and customer-facing roles where empathy drives outcomes.

Key takeaway

VR enables safe, repeatable scenarios that activate the neural pathways for empathy. With the right content, teams can practice perspective-taking and improve real-world interactions.

Source: Society for Neuroscience, ?Virtual reality makes empathy easier,? ScienceDaily, April 20, 2020.
